Acting

EXT. ACTING IS CREATIVITY

Just like there is a certain creativity to coding, I feel like the same type of creativity applies to acting. There is not a certain way to play a character given a scene and circumstances, it is how you believe it should be portrayed. There can be multiple ways.

Of course, certain ways to play a character can be better than others, but that directly relates to coding. You can solve a problem two different ways and one way might take less space and be quicker. Playing a character could be done two ways, and one of the portrayals can help the audience understand more than the other.

Why?

INT./EXT. Why Computer Science? Why Acting?

If I were to be short about it, I guess it's because I love to code, and I love to act. But, there is so much within that love.


I love to act because I kind of get a high off of it, or a release if you will. The goosebumps you get when you do something impressive that you never thought you could do. The entire process of really digging deep into a character and creating a past for the character. The preperation is intense and that just enhances the feeling you get before, during, and after the performance. It's not easy to explain, but it is a beautiful feeling.


I love to code because of nearly the same thing, but in a different form. It's still got a process similar to acting, but it feels quicker to me. Preparing and understanding a problem, then using the tools that you know to solve that problem. It is fulfilling not only because you solved the problem, but because you have another understanding and problem solved under your belt.

You can constantly grow, and that's what both, computer science and acting are about, the chase. The constant want to grow, learn, accomplish. It's something that keep you moving.
